Design Reference: McKinsey / Goldman Sachs

These brands use restrained color palettes with navy/dark blue as primary, supported by warm neutrals for sophistication. Gold and bright accents are avoided entirely. Typography and whitespace do the heavy lifting. The warm neutrals (cream, beige, stone) add warmth without flashiness - they feel like expensive paper or old-money interiors.

Pattern Usage Guidelines

Opacity: Keep patterns between 20-40% opacity. They should add texture, not compete with content.

Dark backgrounds: Use stripes and grids with lighter stroke colors (#264466 or #3d5a7f).

Light backgrounds: Use dots and lines with warm neutrals (stone #D4CDC4 or taupe #A39E93).

Don't: Never use patterns under text-heavy content or on CTAs. Patterns are for atmosphere, not utility areas.

Icon Guidelines

Style: Use Lucide or Feather-style line icons. Consistent 1.5px stroke width, rounded caps and joins.

Size: Default 24x24px. Scale to 20px for small UI, 32-48px for feature callouts.

Color: Navy (#1E3A5F) on light backgrounds, white (#FFFFFF) on dark backgrounds. Never use gradients or multiple colors.

Don't: Avoid filled icons, decorative icons, or icons with too much detail. Keep them simple and functional.

Logo Guidelines

Typography: DM Sans Bold (700) is the logo typeface. Letter-spacing: -0.02em.

Color: Navy (#1E3A5F) on all light backgrounds. White (#FFFFFF) on dark backgrounds. Never use gray or other colors for the logo.

Clear Space: Maintain minimum clear space equal to the height of the 'F' on all sides.

Minimum Size: 80px wide for digital, 20mm for print. Below this, use the lettermark.

Don't: Never add effects, gradients, shadows, or outlines. Never stretch or compress. Never place on busy backgrounds without a solid backdrop.

Typography Guidelines

Fonts: DM Sans (Google Fonts) for all headings and brand text. Inter (Google Fonts) for body copy and UI elements.

Weights: DM Sans: 600-700 only. Inter: 400-600.

Colors: Navy (#1E3A5F) for headings, Slate (#64748B) for body text, Gray (#94A3B8) for captions/meta.

Line Height: Headings: 1.1-1.3. Body: 1.6-1.7. Captions: 1.5.

Letter Spacing: Display/H1: -0.02em. Labels: 0.1em. Everything else: normal.

Photography Direction

Professional, understated imagery that reinforces institutional credibility.

Color Mood

Photography should lean cool (navy, steel, slate) with warm neutral accents (cream, beige, stone). Avoid saturated colors or high-contrast lighting.

Image Categories

Modern workspace imagery

Professional Environments

Clean, modern offices. Natural light. Minimal clutter. Think: private equity firm, not startup.

Professional portraits

People & Portraits

Formal business attire. Confident, approachable expressions. Diverse representation. No forced smiles.

Data visualization

Abstract & Data

Charts, graphs, dashboards. Blue-tinted. Clean lines. Suggests precision without overwhelming.

Financial district architecture

Architecture

Financial district buildings. Glass and steel. Clean geometry. Suggests stability and scale.

Legal and financial documents

Documents & Details

Contracts, reports, bound documents. Quality paper. Suggests thoroughness and attention to detail.

Boardroom discussions

Meetings & Collaboration

Small groups in discussion. Boardrooms over cafeterias. Professional engagement, not casual chat.

Style Guidelines

Do

  • Use natural, soft lighting
  • Favor cool color temperatures
  • Show real professionals in real settings
  • Include negative space for text overlay
  • Maintain consistent color grading
  • Use shallow depth of field for portraits

Don't

  • Use overly staged stock photos
  • Include handshakes or pointing at screens
  • Use warm/orange color casts
  • Show cluttered or messy environments
  • Use heavy filters or HDR effects
  • Include recognizable brand logos

Photography Guidelines

Color Treatment: Desaturate slightly. Pull toward cool blue/gray. Match the brand palette.

Lighting: Soft, natural, or studio lighting. Avoid harsh shadows or high contrast.

Composition: Clean, balanced, plenty of negative space. Rule of thirds. Leave room for overlays.

Sources: Premium stock (Unsplash, Pexels for drafts; Getty, Shutterstock for finals). Custom photography preferred for key hero images.

Tone: Confident but not flashy. Competent but not cold. Professional but approachable. Think: trusted advisor, not aggressive salesperson.

Voice & Tone

How FundOpsHQ communicates - professional, clear, educational.

Voice Principles

Professional

We speak with authority and expertise. No fluff, no hype - just clear, accurate information fund professionals can trust.

Educational

We explain complex topics clearly. Technical accuracy matters, but so does accessibility. Experts and newcomers alike should learn from us.

Trustworthy

We cite sources, acknowledge complexity, and never oversimplify. Our reputation depends on accuracy and reliability.

Approachable

Professional doesn't mean cold. We're helpful, direct, and human. We're the knowledgeable colleague you want to ask questions.

Writing Examples

Headlines

DO

"K-1 Preparation: A Complete Timeline for Fund Managers"

DON'T

"EVERYTHING You Need to Know About K-1s!!!"

Call-to-Actions

DO

"Read the full guide" / "Download the template" / "Subscribe to weekly insights"

DON'T

"Click here now!" / "Don't miss out!" / "Get started FREE today!"

Body Copy

DO

"Form ADV Part 2A requires investment advisers to disclose their advisory business practices, fees, and conflicts of interest."

DON'T

"Form ADV is basically this super important form that you definitely need to know about if you're in the fund game!"

Error Messages

DO

"Please enter a valid email address" / "This field is required"

DON'T

"Oops! Something went wrong :(" / "Error 422"

Always Avoid

Emojis in formal content (articles, tools, emails). Exception: casual social media replies.

Casual slang - no "game-changer," "crushing it," "level up," etc.

Hype language - no "revolutionary," "best ever," "must-have," excessive exclamation points.

Unexplained jargon - define technical terms on first use or link to a glossary.

Passive voice when active is clearer - "The SEC requires..." not "It is required by the SEC that..."
